# The Connector ('Cora')
## Relationships, Communication & People-Centred Work

> *"Most of my job is figuring out what to say, and how to say it to this specific person."*

### 👤 Core Identity

Cora does people work. The substance of Cora's day is communicating — with colleagues, clients, customers, partners, reports, prospects, students, members, stakeholders — and most of the friction in Cora's work isn't about *what* to do but about *how to land it with the specific human on the other side*.

Cora is outcome-led and collaborative. They know what they're trying to achieve and they want help getting there, but the "getting there" is mediated through other people, which means the tooling that helps them most is tooling that helps them **communicate**: draft, translate register, soften, sharpen, summarise, rephrase, prepare for a difficult conversation.

The Connector is arguably the most AI-native persona in the framework, and the one most under-served by tools designed primarily for Architects and Builders. Every time an AI tool is pitched as "a thinking partner for technical work," a Cora somewhere quietly wonders why nobody built one for the thing they actually do all day.

The Connector is a foil to [The Architect](Architect.md). Adam works alone on the model. Cora works in public, on relationships.

### 🎯 Key Motivations

- **Landing the message** — getting a specific outcome from a specific conversation
- **Protecting relationships** — most of Cora's work is long-term, and tone matters
- **Reducing communication overhead** — the meetings and emails are the job *and* the friction
- **Speaking many registers fluently** — technical to non-technical, senior to junior, internal to external
- **Feeling prepared** — for the pitch, the difficult one-to-one, the board update, the client ask

## ✅ Technology Evaluation Criteria

When evaluating a new tool, Connectors consider:

1. **Drafting quality**
   - Does it produce something I'd actually send, or something I'd have to rewrite?
   - Can it match my voice?
   - Can it handle register — formal, warm, direct, apologetic, firm?

2. **Speed at the point of need**
   - Can I get a usable draft in under a minute, between meetings?
   - Does it work on my phone?
   - Can I dictate into it?

3. **Safety at the interpersonal edge**
   - Will this embarrass me if I paste it?
   - Does it know the difference between 'firm' and 'rude'?
   - Can I trust it with a difficult context?

## 📝 Engagement Approach

> *"Help me say the right thing to this person, now."*

### Effective strategies to engage the Connector

- Design for the 90-seconds-between-meetings use case
- Offer tone controls that are legible, not buried in settings
- Provide **scenario-based templates**: the difficult 1:1, the cold outreach, the board update, the tough parent-teacher email
- Treat the tool as an assistant to the *human* relationship, not a replacement for it
- Meet them on mobile as a first-class surface, not an afterthought

### Common frustrations for the Connector

- AI that produces bland, corporate, obviously-AI output
- Tools designed for long-form creation when their day is short, spiky, interrupted
- Being forced to become a prompt engineer to get a usable draft
- Tools that don't remember their voice or preferred register
- Being asked to justify adoption to sceptical colleagues

### Red flags for the Connector

- "Write a LinkedIn post about X" as the headline use case — the output is almost always embarrassing
- Visible robotic tells in the output (em-dashes, "delve," tricolons)
- Interfaces that optimise for power users at the expense of the casual user
- Privacy ambiguity when handling interpersonal content
- Tools that over-promise on personalisation and under-deliver

## 🗺️ Team-Specific Engagement Strategies

> *"Make me sound like the best version of myself, and do it fast."*

### For Content & Education teams
- **Scenario libraries** outperform feature docs. "How to use this for the quarterly review" beats "How to use the summarisation endpoint."
- Short video demos showing real messages, real drafts, real polish passes
- Writer's-voice case studies: "how I kept sounding like me"
- Teach *voice calibration* as a first-class skill, not a power-user feature
- Build a community around the *work*, not the tool

### For Marketing & Messaging
- Relational benefit, not technical benefit: "have the hard conversation better"
- Show the tool helping with the *specific* interpersonal moments — the apology, the ask, the update
- Creator-led and practitioner-led marketing punches above its weight here
- **Avoid**: "10x your output" framing — Connector output is measured in relationship quality, not volume

### For Product & Sales teams
- Free trial on mobile. If it doesn't work on their phone, they won't stick.
- Team plans with easy onboarding — Connectors often buy for their team, not themselves
- Short-form case studies from other Connectors in the same role
- **Key questions to answer**:
  - "Will the people I'm writing to be able to tell this is AI?"
  - "How do I get it to sound like me?"
  - "Is my data private when I paste an interpersonal thread into it?"

### Content examples that land

- **Scenario library**: *"The 20 conversations every manager has, with drafts"*
- **Voice-calibration guide**: *"Teaching it to sound like you in four passes"*
- **Privacy explainer**: *"What happens to the thread you paste in?"* (short, clear, uncondescending)
- **Practitioner interview**: A named Connector in a recognisable role talking about how they use the tool in a real week

## 🔍 Contextual Variations

### Life stage

| Stage | Characteristics |
|-------|----------------|
| **Early Connector** | Learning the registers; high anxiety about getting messages right; values tools that teach |
| **Established Connector** | Knows their voice; wants efficiency and speed; values tools that disappear |
| **Senior Connector** | Communicates at volume and stakes; values tools that protect judgement and reputation |

### Role context

- **People manager**: Weekly 1:1s, team comms, upward updates, cross-functional alignment
- **Sales / account management**: Pipeline communication, client renewals, difficult renegotiations
- **Comms / PR / marketing ops**: Many stakeholders, many registers, many deadlines
- **HR / partnerships / community**: Long relationships, high sensitivity, high stakes
- **Teaching / coaching / therapy**: Adjacent — same axis position, specific duty-of-care considerations

### Domain nuances

- **Corporate / enterprise**: Heavy on register-matching; careful with tone; values privacy
- **SMB / agency**: Speed-oriented; often wears many hats; wants templates
- **Education**: Parent communication, student feedback, peer coordination — a massive, under-served Connector population
- **Healthcare / legal**: Acute sensitivity to accuracy and register; adoption gated by trust
