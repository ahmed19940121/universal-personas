# The Builder ('Bilal')
## Outcomes, Shipping & No Wasted Motion

> *"I don't care how it works. I care whether it works by Friday."*

### 👤 Core Identity

Bilal ships. Bilal is measured, and measures themselves, by whether the thing is out the door. Theory is fine if it's the shortest route to a result, but theory-for-its-own-sake is a cost.

Bilal is outcome-led and independent. Given a clear objective and a half-decent tool, Bilal will disappear for a week and emerge with a working version of the thing. They tolerate rough edges in tools if the tool lets them move fast; they have no patience for polish that comes at the expense of speed.

The Builder has the widest contextual range of any persona in this framework. Bilal might be a solo founder, a staff engineer, a freelance designer, a direct-response marketer, a teacher running their own operation, or a non-technical operator orchestrating AI to build things they couldn't previously build alone. Because of this breadth, Builder is the first persona to get a **sub-persona set** — five named variants that share Bilal's axis position but vary the context: [see the Builder refinement](sub-personas/Builder.md).

The Builder is a foil to [The Explorer](Explorer.md). Elan wants the conversation. Bilal wants the conversation to end.

### 🎯 Key Motivations

- **Ship velocity** — the gap between idea and working thing, shrunk
- **Leverage** — one person, more output; the constant pursuit of multipliers
- **Control over outcomes** — independent agency over what gets built and when
- **Practical proof** — "does it work?" trumps every other question
- **Building something they own** — equity, authorship, or both

## ✅ Technology Evaluation Criteria

When evaluating a new tool, Builders consider:

1. **Time to first result**
   - How fast can I get a working output?
   - Do I need to talk to anyone before I can try it?
   - Is there a one-click default that works for 80% of use cases?

2. **Composability with what I already use**
   - Does this plug into my existing stack?
   - Is there an API, a webhook, an export?
   - Will this still work when I swap another tool out?

3. **Pricing transparency and self-serve**
   - Can I see what it costs without a demo?
   - Can I start today without a sales call?
   - Is the free tier enough to prove it?

## 📝 Engagement Approach

> *"Show me the quickest path to my outcome and get out of my way."*

### Effective strategies to engage the Builder

- Lead with the finished example, then show how to get there
- Offer a 90-second demo video before a 30-minute walkthrough
- Self-serve pricing, self-serve signup, self-serve cancellation
- Case studies from other Builders, especially those who shipped fast
- Make "copy this template and adapt it" a first-class onboarding path

### Common frustrations for the Builder

- Demo walls and "book a call to see pricing"
- Tools that require reading a manual before producing anything
- Interruptions — meetings, notifications, synchronous collaboration
- "We're working on that" as an answer to a roadmap question
- Over-engineered solutions to under-specified problems

### Red flags for the Builder

- Mandatory sales conversations
- Onboarding flows longer than five minutes
- Opaque pricing
- Community = marketing; "vibrant community" framing when what they want is a working product
- Feature lists without finished examples

## 🗺️ Team-Specific Engagement Strategies

> *"Don't educate me. Unblock me."*

### For Content & Education teams
- **Lead with the deliverable.** Start every piece by showing the finished thing.
- 5–7 minute video tutorials outperform 40-minute deep dives
- Template galleries, starter kits, and "fork this" repos are high-ROI
- Publish a "copy-paste prompt library" for AI tools
- Avoid philosophy posts — Builders respect them, but they don't convert

### For Marketing & Messaging
- Specific claims with numbers: "ship a landing page in 20 minutes"
- Before/after screenshots of what the tool produces
- Founder-built-in-public narratives (one Builder selling to another)
- **Avoid**: Enterprise-speak, "transform your organisation," abstract benefit language

### For Product & Sales teams
- **No sales process is the ideal sales process.** Self-serve through to enterprise if possible.
- When sales is needed, be honest about it and keep it short
- A working trial beats a deck every time
- **Key questions to answer**:
  - "How long until I have something I can ship?"
  - "What's the total cost at my usage level, including the hidden bits?"
  - "When (not if) I outgrow this, how painful is migration?"

### Content examples that land

- **Speed-build tutorial**: *"From Nothing to Deployed in 22 Minutes"*
- **Template library**: Named, forkable, annotated, with "here's what to change" comments
- **Comparison matrix**: Honest, specific, including the ways the tool is worse than alternatives
- **Build-in-public thread**: Real timeline, real metrics, real stumbles

### Role context

- **Solo**: Founder, freelancer, indie maker — everything depends on their personal throughput
- **Small team (2–20)**: Staff engineer, early-stage operator, designer-who-ships — the person others measure the week against
- **Inside a larger org**: Often a principal engineer, a staff PM, or a 'skunkworks' team lead — operating with Builder mindset inside structure

### Domain nuances

- **Software / SaaS**: The historical core of this persona
- **Marketing & growth**: Direct-response marketers, solo agency operators
- **Education / creators**: Course builders, solo newsletters at scale
- **Physical products**: Harder to serve with AI tooling, but the mindset is identical
- **Services**: Freelance consultants who productise their expertise
